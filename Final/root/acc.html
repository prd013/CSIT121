What is ACC

ACC stands for Asian Cricket Council. It coordinates cricket in Asia. It currently consists of 25 member associations. Jay Shah is the current president of Asian Cricket Council. Pankaj Khimji the vice-president is also the president of Oman Cricket Team.

History

The council was formed as the Asian Cricket Conference in New Delhi, India, on 19 September 1983, with the original members being Bangladesh, India, Malaysia, Pakistan, Singapore, and Sri Lanka. Changing its name to the present in 1995. Until 2003, the headquarters of the council were rotated biennially amongst the presidents' and secretaries' home countries. The organisation's current president is Jay Shah, who is also the Secretary of the BCCI.

ACC Events

Asia Cup

Asia Cup is a 50 over and 20 over format games held once every 2-4 years. The first Asia cup was held in 1983, the latest is going to be held in September 2023 in Pakistan. India and Sri Lanka are the most successful teams, with each teams reaching finals 10 and 12 times respectively.

In order to play the asia cup team has to qualify through a tournament named as"ACC Emerging Teams Asia Cup". The winner of the tournament plays Asia Cup. Hong Kong has played the most number of Asia Cups among the associate nations surpassing Afghanistan.
